Rather than haphazardly following good advice, I've found it useful to develop a system of memorable, wide-ranging rules, and to take those rules very seriously.
These rules were chosen as valuable principles that I need to be frequently reminded of or that sometimes go against my natural tendencies.

  1. Escape Competition
  2. Keep Your Identity Small
  3. Find Everything Interesting
  4. Make Things
  5. Seek Variance
  6. Synthesize
  7. Read, Write, and Code, a lot
  8. Acquire Leverage
  9. Practice Wonder, Gratitude, Peace, and Compersion
  10. Move Fast and be Patient
  11. Love All
  12. ... tbd

Notes: 1. Thiel on Girard. Mimetic Theory is fairly popular now, but most think of it as another useful mental model, when in reality, it's a much more profound insight. 2. Keep Your Identity Small 3. "Everything is interesting" - Tyler Cowen 4. From here, among other places 6. Pretty common idea but I've always enjoyed this mental model 8. Find a position of leverage
